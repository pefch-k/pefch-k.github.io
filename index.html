About

I am a final-year student at the Department of Mathematics of the Aristotle University of Thessaloniki. My academic interests focus primarily on algebra and computer science, with a particular emphasis on mathematical logic. Through my studies I've developed a capacity for abstract thinking and rigorous analysis, and I keep pushing at the edges of what I currently understand.

During a cybersecurity internship I worked directly with modern corporate IT environments, gaining hands-on experience with the structure of operating systems — which broadened my perspective on formal systems more generally. That experience fed directly into my current work with the Lean proof assistant: I'd already partially mastered pieces of both disciplines that underpin it.

My goal, from the start of my academic path, has stayed the same: keep developing my skills, and keep exploring the edge of what I know.

2×2 Sudoku

A constraint-satisfaction Sudoku engine written in SWI-Prolog, compiled to WASM and running entirely client-side. Generates puzzles with a provably unique solution and checks/solves boards live.

MacWilliams Identity — Formalized in Lean

A formal verification of the MacWilliams Identity for binary linear codes: proves that the weight enumerator of a code determines that of its orthogonal dual, using Mathlib's Submodule and Hamming-space machinery.
